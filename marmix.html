01.Overview

Marmix is a local concrete manufacturer serving both trade and individual customers. They needed a website that felt as reliable and modern as the products they deliver — one that could clearly present their offer and make ordering construction materials simple for everyday (B2C) buyers, not just industry insiders.

I led a full redesign focused on a centered hero layout, a clear product structure, and a streamlined ordering experience that removes friction from the first click to a placed request.

✎ Replace with the real story: who Marmix is, why they came to you, and what success looked like for them.

02.Problem

The previous website made it hard for regular customers to understand the product range and how to place an order. Information was scattered, the ordering path was unclear, and the design didn't build the trust a construction buyer needs before committing to a purchase.

✎ Add the specific pain points you found — from a client brief, an audit, or user feedback (e.g. confusing navigation, no mobile support, unclear pricing).

03.Design Process

I started with market and competitor research to understand how construction suppliers present their offer, then mapped the ordering journey for a typical B2C buyer. From there I moved into low-fidelity wireframes to test the structure before committing to visuals.

Once the flow felt right, I designed high-fidelity screens with a centered hero that leads straight into the core message, a clean product catalog, and a simplified ordering step. I built the layout mobile-first so buyers can order from the site wherever they are.
